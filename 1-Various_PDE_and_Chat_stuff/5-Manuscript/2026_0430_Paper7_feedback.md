# Important Modeling Guidance

A major takeaway from the meeting:

> Do not directly place every proposed mechanism into the formal model.

Too many explicit mechanisms would risk:
- overfitting,
- non-identifiability,
- interpretive ambiguity,
- and unnecessary complexity.

Instead, the recommended structure increasingly appears to be:

| Layer | Role |
| :--- | :--- |
| Minimal model | distinction scarcity + local pool quality |
| Mechanism interpretation | development, evaluator compression, congestion |
| Network extensions | talent paradox, hierarchical distance |
| Future work | endogenous sorting, topology, flow dynamics |

This architecture preserves theoretical richness while maintaining model tractability.

The proposed “peer talent paradox” received stronger resonance than expected.

Potential formulation:

\[
E[T_{peer}] > E[T]
\]

inside career-relevant exposure networks.

More specifically:

\[
\textbf{

}
\]

This creates the possibility that:

This may have:
- developmental consequences,
- evaluative consequences,
- and psychological consequences.

- explanatory,
