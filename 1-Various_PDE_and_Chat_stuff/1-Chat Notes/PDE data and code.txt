2. UNIT IDENTIFICATION CODES (UICS)
a. Format - In the Army, each organizational unit is given a Unit Identification Code (UIC).  Since the Army is a hierarchical organization, these UICs are designed so that they nest.  UICs are 6-character codes that consist of prefixes (the first 4 digits) and endings (the last 2 digits). Usually the prefixes represent a battalion-sized organization.  An Army battalion usually consists of 2-6 “line” companies and a headquarters company. The line companies for a given battalion prefix will end in A0, B0, C0, D0, E0, or F0 representing “A” Company, “B” Company, etc. The headquarters and headquarters company, also called an HHC, always ends in T0.   
b. For Example – 1st Battalion, 501st Infantry Regt is a battalion element commonly called 1-501.  The UIC for the battalion HQ is WH9RAA.  The battalion will have a “HQ and HQ Company” (HHC) and its UIC will be WH9RT0, Alpha Company will be WH9RA0, etc.  These are the UNSCRAMBELED UICs that I am showing you.  The scrambled UICs in the master_data_pde table also have prefixes and suffixes.  They are 6 and 4 digits respectively, and they are consistent throughout the table and schema.
