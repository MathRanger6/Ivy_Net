# Replication Data Search: Pool Quality and Promotion

## Overview

This document summarizes publicly available datasets, papers, and
repositories suitable for replicating findings on how **peer pool
quality affects promotion outcomes** in hierarchical systems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## I. Key Insight

Across labor economics, public administration, and education: - Many
datasets include **performance ratings + hierarchical advancement** -
The closest analogs involve: - Manager-based evaluation systems -
Tournament-style promotions - Cohort-based advancement (schools,
bureaucracies)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## II. Recommended Public Datasets

### 1. U.S. OPM FedScope / EHRI

-   Access: https://www.opm.gov/data/
-   Performance: Grade / pay progression
-   Pool: Agency × unit × year
-   Outcome: Promotion (grade increases)
-   Limitation: No direct ratings

### 2. LEHD (Census)

-   Access: https://lehd.ces.census.gov/
-   Performance: Earnings growth
-   Pool: Firm × establishment
-   Outcome: Job mobility / promotion
-   Limitation: No ratings

### 3. NLSY97

-   Access: https://www.bls.gov/nls/
-   Performance: Test scores, wages
-   Pool: Employer / occupation
-   Outcome: Promotions
-   Limitation: Weak pool definition

### 4. Add Health

-   Access: https://addhealth.cpc.unc.edu/
-   Performance: GPA, test scores
-   Pool: School × cohort
-   Outcome: College admission
-   Limitation: Educational setting

### 5. Florida Teacher Data

-   Access: via replication packages
-   Performance: Value-added scores
-   Pool: School × grade
-   Outcome: Promotion / reassignment

### 6. IPEDS + OpenAlex

-   Access: https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/ + https://openalex.org/
-   Performance: Publications, citations
-   Pool: Department × institution
-   Outcome: Promotion (inferred)

### 7. Sports Data (NBA, etc.)

-   Access: Kaggle / sports-reference
-   Performance: Player stats
-   Pool: Team × season
-   Outcome: Selection / advancement

## VI. Conclusion

Best replication opportunities: 1. Firm personnel datasets\
2. Teacher administrative data\
3. Bureaucratic promotion data\
4. Academic career datasets

These settings best match: - Defined pools\
- Relative evaluation\
- Competitive advancement
